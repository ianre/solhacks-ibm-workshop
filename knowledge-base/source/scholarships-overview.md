# Scholarships Overview

Workshop note: This document is sample content for the Becas y Recursos workshop. It is meant for demo use only and is not official advising.

## Section 1: FAFSA and federal aid basics

The FAFSA is the Free Application for Federal Student Aid. It is often the first step students take before they apply for grants, loans, and many scholarships. For this workshop sample, students should aim for a priority filing date of March 1 for the next academic year. The federal FAFSA deadline is listed here as June 30 at the end of the award year. Schools and states may use earlier priority dates, so students should always verify the real deadline with official sources.

Students may qualify for federal aid even if they do not think of themselves as "financial aid students." Completing the FAFSA can open access to Pell Grants, work-study, subsidized loans, and some campus-based emergency support funds.

## Section 2: Pell Grant snapshot

The Pell Grant is a federal grant for undergraduate students with financial need. In this workshop sample, Pell Grant awards are described as need-based and do not need to be repaid if the student remains eligible. Award amounts vary by year, enrollment status, and Student Aid Index rules in effect for that cycle.

Students should know three things about Pell Grants:

- They usually require a completed FAFSA
- Part-time students may still qualify, but the amount can be lower
- Pell Grant eligibility can help a student qualify for additional campus or nonprofit aid

## Section 3: Hispanic Scholarship Fund

The Hispanic Scholarship Fund, or HSF, supports students of Hispanic heritage pursuing undergraduate or graduate education. In this workshop sample, the scholarship application opens in January and uses a sample deadline of February 15.

Sample eligibility details in this workshop:

- Student identifies as Hispanic heritage
- Student plans to enroll full-time at an accredited college or university
- Student demonstrates academic achievement and leadership
- Student submits transcripts, a personal statement, and basic financial information

Sample award range: $500 to $5,000 depending on need and available funding.

## Section 4: LULAC National Scholarship Fund

The League of United Latin American Citizens, or LULAC, offers scholarships through local councils and partner organizations. In this workshop sample, the deadline is March 31.

Sample eligibility details:

- Open to graduating high school seniors, community college students, four-year college students, and graduate students
- Leadership, service, and academic effort are all considered
- Some local programs may ask for essays or recommendation letters

Sample award range: $250 to $2,000.

## Section 5: HSI Student Success Grant

This workshop also includes a fictional campus-based scholarship called the HSI Student Success Grant. It is used to show how school-specific aid can appear alongside national scholarships.

Sample details:

- For students enrolled at a Hispanic-Serving Institution
- Preference for first-generation students and Pell-eligible students
- Sample application window: April 1 to May 15
- Requires a short statement about academic goals and campus involvement
- Sample award amount: up to $1,500 for the next term

## Section 6: Smart application strategy

Students should not wait for a single "perfect" scholarship. A stronger strategy is to apply for multiple scholarships across campus, nonprofit, federal, and professional sources. Students should keep a simple tracker with:

- Scholarship name
- Deadline
- Required materials
- Essay prompt
- Recommendation status
- Submission date

Starting early matters. A student who begins one month before deadlines has more time to request letters, revise essays, and check eligibility details.
