# Campus Resources FAQ

Workshop note: This document is sample content for the Becas y Recursos workshop. It is meant for demo use only and is not official university policy.

## FAQ 1: Is the food pantry confidential?

Yes. In this workshop sample, pantry visits are treated as confidential support visits. Staff members are expected to focus on helping students access food and essentials without judgment.

## FAQ 2: How do I get emergency funds?

Students should start with the Office of Student Support emergency aid form. In this workshop sample, the process includes:

1. Submitting the online form
2. Explaining the urgent need
3. Uploading any requested documentation
4. Watching for follow-up from a case manager

Routine decisions are described as taking two to five business days.

## FAQ 3: Can I get tutoring for any class?

Not every course has a tutor every week. In this workshop sample, tutoring is reliably available for math, writing, chemistry, biology, and introductory programming. Students who need help in other subjects should still ask the Learning Commons because availability changes by semester.

## FAQ 4: What if I need help after hours?

In this workshop sample:

- Mental health emergencies should use the 24/7 crisis line at 555-0199
- Non-emergency tutoring questions can wait for the Learning Commons to reopen
- Students with urgent housing, food, or safety concerns should contact campus police or the emergency support line listed by their institution

## FAQ 5: Do I need insurance to use student health services?

This workshop sample says basic clinic visits are included in student fees, but outside referrals, labs, and prescriptions may involve additional costs. Students should confirm current coverage rules with the health clinic before an appointment.

## FAQ 6: Do I need an appointment for counseling or tutoring?

In this workshop sample:

- Counseling appointments can be booked by phone or through the wellness portal
- Crisis appointments may be available the same day
- Math and writing tutoring allow drop-ins
- Science and programming tutoring work better by appointment
