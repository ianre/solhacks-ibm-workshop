# Scholarship FAQ

Workshop note: This document is sample content for the Becas y Recursos workshop. It is meant for demo use only and is not official advising.

## FAQ 1: What GPA do I need?

There is no single GPA rule for every scholarship. In this workshop sample, many opportunities use one of three patterns:

- Minimum 2.5 GPA for access-focused scholarships
- Minimum 3.0 GPA for general merit scholarships
- Minimum 3.5 GPA for highly competitive awards

Students with lower GPAs should still apply for need-based and community-based opportunities because many programs look at leadership, persistence, work experience, and service.

## FAQ 2: Can undocumented students apply?

Some scholarships do not require U.S. citizenship, but others do. Students should read each eligibility section closely. In this workshop sample:

- The HSI Student Success Grant does not require citizenship language in the sample description
- The HSF sample requires students to verify the exact rules with official sources
- Federal aid tied to the FAFSA may have citizenship or eligible noncitizen requirements

The safest guidance is to verify each scholarship individually and connect students with an official campus financial aid office when status questions come up.

## FAQ 3: Do I need an essay?

Many scholarships ask for a short personal statement. The most common essay themes in this workshop sample are:

- Educational goals
- Leadership and service
- Financial need
- Community impact
- Why the student should be selected

Students should keep one strong base essay and adapt it for each application instead of starting from zero every time.

## FAQ 4: Can I apply for more than one scholarship?

Yes. Students should usually apply for as many scholarships as they are realistically eligible for. Applying to multiple scholarships increases the chance of receiving support and helps students build a reusable application package.

A reusable package usually includes:

- Resume
- Unofficial transcript
- Personal statement
- List of activities and service
- Contact information for recommenders

## FAQ 5: When should I start applying?

Students should begin gathering materials four to six weeks before the earliest deadline. In this workshop sample:

- HSF sample deadline: February 15
- LULAC sample deadline: March 31
- HSI Student Success Grant sample window: April 1 to May 15
- FAFSA sample priority date: March 1

Because real dates change each year, students should verify them on official websites before they submit.

## FAQ 6: What if I miss a deadline?

Missing one deadline does not mean all funding is lost. Students can still:

- Look for later-cycle scholarships
- Ask the financial aid office about emergency aid
- Check with their college department for smaller program awards
- Prepare early for the next cycle

The key message is to stay in motion. One missed deadline should not stop the search.
